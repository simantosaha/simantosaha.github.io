SIMANTO SAHA (Member, IEEE) received the Master’s of Philosophy degree from The University of Adelaide, with the Dean’s Commendation for Master’s by Research Thesis Excellence, and the Ph.D. degree from the School of Biomedical Engineering, The University of Sydney. He has co-authored ten journal articles and 14 conference papers/abstracts. According to Google Scholar, the total citations of his works are more than 1000, and the H-Index is eight. His research interests include medical technologies, digital signal processing, artificial intelligence, and brain–computer interface. He received the Leo J. Mahar Scholarship from the University of Adelaide for pursuing his research. He was awarded the Research Travel Scholarship from The University of Adelaide and the IEEE EMBS Student Travel Award for attending IEEE EMBC 2019. He has been affiliated with IEEE, since 2013, and served as the News and Communication Coordinator in the IEEE EMBS Bangladesh Chapter, in 2016. He was a recipient of the University of Sydney International Scholarship. He also received the APR Intern Program Scholarship for an Internship at Dolby Australia. He was a Reviewer in selected journals, including Scientific Reports, IEEE TRANSACTIONS ON NEURAL SYSTEMS AND REHABILITATION ENGINEERING, IEEE ACCESS, and Biomedical Signal Processing and Control.
